ATLANTA — Starting this week, you may hop into an Uber in Atlanta and find no driver behind the wheel — a first-of-its-kind experience now exclusive to the city.

Uber has officially launched its driverless rideshare service in Atlanta through a partnership with Waymo, bringing fully autonomous, all-electric vehicles to local streets. The rollout features Waymo’s Jaguar I-PACE robotaxis, operating across a 65-square-mile zone from Buckhead to Capitol View.

“We’re thrilled to continue deepening our partnership with Waymo as we expand autonomous rides to Atlanta,” said Sarfraz Maredia, Uber’s Head of Autonomous Mobility & Delivery.

Atlanta is now one of only a few U.S. cities offering public self-driving Uber rides, joining places like Phoenix and Austin.

How to ride a Waymo in Atlanta

Uber riders in Atlanta can now be matched with a Waymo robotaxi when selecting UberX, Uber Comfort, or Uber Comfort Electric. Rides in the driverless Waymo vehicles cost the same as standard Uber rides, with no extra charge for using an autonomous vehicle.

Want to increase your chances of getting a robotaxi?

  • Open the Uber app and go to Settings
  • Tap Ride Preferences
  • Toggle on “Autonomous Vehicles” under preferences

You’ll receive a notification before being matched with a Waymo and can either accept the ride or choose a traditional Uber driver. Once your Waymo self-driving vehicle arrives, you’ll unlock the doors, pop the trunk (if you need it), hop in and start the ride all from your Uber app.

Credit: Uber x Waymo
Uber app shows real-time support and control when you ride with a Waymo car in Atlanta.

Where robotaxis operate in Atlanta

Waymo’s self-driving Ubers run from Buckhead to Lakewood Heights, covering 65 square miles in Atlanta including: 

  • Buckhead
  • Midtown
  • Downtown
  • Capitol View and nearby neighborhoods

Note: These vehicles do not drive on interstates or pick up at Atlanta's airport (ATL) at this time.

Is it safe to ride in a self-driving Uber in Atlanta?

Waymo states that its Atlanta fleet utilizes the latest version of its 5th-generation Automated Driving System, which was updated in late 2024 following a federal recall.

That recall involved 16 minor collisions with gates and barriers, no injuries were reported and all affected vehicles were fixed by December 2024. The current software is designed to better detect and avoid chains, gates, and road barriers, according to a May 2025 filing with the NHTSA.

“Atlantans are now able to experience the same safety, comfort and convenience of Waymo’s fully autonomous driving technology that millions... currently have access to,” added Nicole Gavel, Head of Business Development at Waymo.

Meanwhile, researchers at Georgia Tech’s Autonomous and Connected Transportation Lab are studying how self-driving cars perform in Atlanta’s complex traffic, weather shifts, and local driving habits.

Credit: Uber X Waymo
Waymo’s self-driving Ubers run from Buckhead to Lakewood Heights, covering 65 square miles in Atlanta.


What to expect during a driverless ride in Atlanta

  • Same pricing as UberX and Uber Comfort
  • No tipping option for autonomous rides
  • 24/7 human support available inside the car and via the app
  • Riders can still rate the ride like any other Uber trip

Uber and Waymo are working with Atlanta-based partner Avomo to manage fleet maintenance, charging and cleaning. Waymo handles roadside assistance, vehicle testing and safety monitoring.

What’s next for Waymo and Uber in Atlanta?

Uber said it now supports over 1.5 million autonomous trips annually and plans to grow the number of robotaxis in Atlanta over the next few years. A similar expansion is underway in Austin.

In other cities, Waymo rides are rated 4.9 stars on average.

Try it today: 

  1. Make sure your Uber app is updated
  2. Go to Settings > Ride Preferences
  3. Turn on Autonomous Vehicle Matching

You’ll be notified in-app or by email if you're selected for an AV ride.
